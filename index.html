Decades of Genius: Tony Stark's Timeline

  • 1970: Born in Manhattan, New York to Howard Stark and Maria Stark
  • Early Life: Born on May 29, 1970, in Manhattan; raised by Howard and Maria Stark with the guidance of Edwin Jarvis; attends MIT at a young age
  • 1991: Loses parents in an assassination; inherits Stark Industries; becomes known for weapons design and playboy lifestyle
  • 1999: Co-founds Stark Industries
  • 2008: Builds the first Iron Man suit in captivity, marking the beginning of his superhero journey
  • Mark I (2008): Improvised suit built to escape captivity
  • Mark II (2010): Silver streamlined suit, designed for high-altitude flight
  • Mark III (2008): Red and gold suit, first fully integrated version with advanced weaponry
  • Mark IV (2010): Enhanced version with improved durability and capabilities
  • Mark V (2010): Portable suitcase suit for quick deployment
  • 2010: Unveils the Stark Expo, showcasing his commitment to clean energy and technology
  • Mark VI (2012): Upgraded armor with advanced features and improved flight stability
  • Mark VII (2012): Features remote control capabilities and responds to mental commands
  • Mark XLII (2013): Modular armor with individual pieces controlled remotely
  • Mark XLIII (2015): Improved version with enhanced strength and durability
  • Iron Legion (2015): Autonomous drone army created for peacekeeping
  • Mark XLIV (2015): Hulkbuster armor designed to combat the Hulk
  • Mark XLV (2015): Enhanced suit with improved flight capabilities
  • 2012: Forms the Avengers to combat global threats alongside other superheroes
  • 2013: Develops PTSD from the Battle of New York; creates numerous Iron Man suits; faces the Mandarin's attacks
  • Mark XLVI (2016): Continued advancements with enhanced defense mechanisms
  • Mark XLVII (2017): Nanotech suit with the ability to materialize on Tony's body
  • Mark L (2018): Nanotech suit with numerous enhancements, part of the Bleeding Edge armor
  • 2015: Creates Ultron, an AI program with unintended consequences, leading to global conflict
  • Mark LXXXV (2023): Final iteration used in the Battle of Earth, equipped with nanotech and specialized weaponry
  • 2013: Destroys his armors as a symbolic gesture, undergoing a period of personal change
  • 2015: Raids Hydra facility; inadvertently creates Ultron; battles the sentient AI and faces consequences
  • 2016: Supports oversight; assembles a team to capture renegades; confronts Captain America at Leipzig/Halle Airport
  • 2016: Discovers Bucky Barnes was framed; truce with Captain America; learns about his parents' death; temporarily paralyzed
  • 2017: Creates the "Bleeding Edge" armor; mentors Peter Parker/Spider-Man; battles Vulture
  • 2018: Receives a warning about Thanos; confronts the mad Titan with the Avengers; stabs by Thanos on Titan
  • 2018: Rescued from space by Carol Danvers; chooses to retire and raise his daughter Morgan with Pepper Potts
  • 2023: Revisits past during the time heist; interacts with a younger version of his father Howard Stark
  • 2023: Sacrifices himself wielding the Infinity Stones to save the universe; dies surrounded by friends and family
  • 2023: Leaves a holographic message for his daughter Morgan Stark, guiding her on life without him
  • 2023: Legacy lives on as Morgan Stark inherits his technology and mantle
  • 2023: His funeral is attended by a multitude of friends and allies
  • Unknown: Time-travels to the 1970s during the Avengers' mission to retrieve the Infinity Stones
  • Unknown: Interacts with his father Howard Stark in the past, gaining insights into his upbringing
  • Unknown: Successfully retrieves the Space Stone during the time heist
  • Unknown: His actions contribute to the defeat of Thanos in an alternate timeline
  • Unknown: Returns to the present but succumbs to the injuries sustained during the time heist
  • Unknown: Leaves a profound impact on the Avengers and the world, remembered as a hero
  • Unknown: Pepper Potts establishes the Stark Foundation to continue Tony Stark's philanthropic work
  • Unknown: Inspires a new generation of heroes and scientists
  • Unknown: Immortalized in holographic memorials and in the hearts of those he touched
  • Legacy: Eight months later, Peter Parker receives Stark's glasses and message, establishing him as Stark's chosen successor

"He [Tony Stark] was an industrialist and a bit of a playboy. He was a jack of all trades, but he was a millionaire who made weapons. We thought it would be interesting to take a guy who was not very heroic at all and see if we could make him a hero."

--Don Heck (Co-creator of Iron Man)
